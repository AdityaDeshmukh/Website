About me

I was a PhD candidate in ECE Department at UIUC, advised by Prof. Venu Veeravalli. I successfully defended my PhD thesis in December 2023. Before coming to UIUC, I graduated with a Dual Degree (B.Tech & M.Tech) in Electrical Engineering from IIT Madras, advised by Prof. Srikrishna Bhashyam. Under my undergraduate studies, I had the pleasure of working with Prof. Rahul Vaze at TIFR.

Research Interests

My research interests broadly lie in the areas of statistical inference, machine learning, reinforcement learning, optimization and information theory. In my research, I focus on discovering and understanding fundamental limits of learning and decision-making problems through theoretical analysis, and on design and development of novel algorithms which achieve these limits with provable guarantees. I am passionate about studying and developing tools which enable us to solve problems in a principled manner. My research projects lie in the diverse fields of packet scheduling, multi-armed bandits, controlled sensing, robust inference, network optimization, and multi-modal distributed feature compression. Though the goals of these projects are varied, the focus is upon providing guarantees on the vital engineering criteria of optimality, efficiency and robustness. I strive to recognize and solve open problems which have significant practical scope and applicability, and which require development and analysis of theoretical underpinnings.
